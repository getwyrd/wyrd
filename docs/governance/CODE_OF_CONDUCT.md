# Code of Conduct

> **Status: DRAFT.** Adapted from the [Apache Software Foundation Code of Conduct](https://www.apache.org/foundation/policies/conduct.html). The project's own **Values** (below) are still **TBD**, and the reporting contact must be filled in before ratification. Referenced by [GOVERNANCE.md](GOVERNANCE.md).

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to everyone who takes part in the Wyrd project — contributors, associates, maintainers, and the architecture board — in every project space: repositories, issues and pull requests, discussions, chat, and any event held in the project's name.

## Our expectations

In the spirit of the Apache Software Foundation's Code of Conduct, we ask everyone in the community to be guided by the following:

- **Be open.** We welcome anyone who wishes to take part, in any role, and we make decisions in the open.
- **Be considerate.** Your work will be used by others, and you depend on the work of others; the decisions you make affect people, so weigh the consequences.
- **Be respectful.** Disagreement is no excuse for poor manners. We treat one another with respect, especially when we differ.
- **Be collaborative.** Work openly, prefer transparency, and share early so others can help and build on what you do.
- **Be careful in the words you choose.** We are a community of professionals. Be kind; do not insult, demean, harass, or exclude others.
- **When we disagree, try to understand why.** Seek to understand other viewpoints before resolving a disagreement; differences are an asset, to be worked through constructively.

## Unacceptable behaviour

Harassment, discrimination, personal attacks, sustained disruption, publishing others' private information, and any conduct that would reasonably make others feel unsafe or unwelcome are not tolerated, in any project space.

## Diversity statement

We welcome and encourage participation by everyone, and are committed to being a community in which all participants feel safe — regardless of background or identity. Differences of viewpoint, experience, and identity make the project stronger.

## Reporting and enforcement

If you believe someone is violating this Code of Conduct, report it to **TBD** — a confidential contact to be designated before ratification. Reports are handled discreetly. The maintainers and the architecture board are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this Code and may take any action they deem appropriate, up to and including removal from the project.
